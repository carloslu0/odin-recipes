Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang is a classic Filipino soup characterized by its sour and savory medley of flavors. It’s popular comfort food in the Philippines, usually served on its own or paired with steamed rice on rainy days to ward off the cold

Like adobo, the term sinigang describes more a cooking method than a particular dish as it comes with many variations. It can be made with protein such as pork, fish, shrimp, beef, and chicken and souring agents such as tamarind, guava, green mango, calamansi, kamias, batuan, santol, and other native fruits.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly or buto-buto
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • 3 tbsps fish sauce
  • 12 pieces string beans cut in 2 inch length
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 3 pieces chili
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 piece onion sliced
  • 2 pieces taro gabi quartered
  • 1 pack sinigang mix

Steps

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil.
  2. Sauté the onion until its layers separate from each other.
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown.
  4. Put in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients.
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil.
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender.
  7. Put in the sinigang mix and chili.
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes.
  9. Put in the spinach, turn off the heat, and cover the pot. Let the spinach cook using the remaining heat in the pot.
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
